High-Precision Airtightness Testing Solution for GPS Products

Overview of GPS Airtightness Testing

Global Positioning System (GPS) products are widely used in vehicles, ships, aviation, outdoor sports, and other fields. These devices often operate in harsh environments, including high/low temperatures, humidity, and dust. Therefore, the airtightness performance of GPS products directly affects their reliability and service life.

The GPS airtightness testing solution provided by WAFU Brothers adopts high-precision leak detection technology, capable of quickly and accurately testing the sealing performance of GPS products, ensuring they meet IP67 or higher protection standards.

High-Precision Testing Solution

Based on the characteristics of GPS products, we offer various airtightness testing methods, including direct pressure testing, differential pressure testing, and helium mass spectrometry leak detection. The most suitable solution can be selected according to product type and requirements.

Testing Process & Steps

1

Product Preparation

Clean the GPS product surface to remove dust, grease, or other contaminants. Place the product on the testing station and secure it in place.

2

Fixture Installation

Select suitable fixtures based on the product shape, install and adjust them to ensure proper contact and a sealed chamber.

3

Parameter Setup

Set parameters on the test device, including pressure, inflation time, balance time, and test duration. Define pass/fail criteria based on airtightness standards.

4

Airtightness Testing

Start the program. The system automatically inflates, balances, and tests the product. During the process, the pressure in the chamber is monitored to calculate the leak rate.

5

Result Judgment

After the test, the system compares the result with the set standard to determine if the product passes and displays the result.

6

Data Logging & Traceability

The system automatically records each test, including time, pressure, leak rate, and result. Data can be stored in a database for future queries and traceability.
