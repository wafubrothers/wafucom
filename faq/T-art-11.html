3. Future Technology Development Trends

With deepening industrial digital transformation, high-precision airtightness testing technology is expected to develop in three main directions.

1. "Multi-Physical Field Fusion" of Detection Dimensions

Single pressure parameter detection will upgrade to multidimensional analysis including pressure, temperature, and humidity:

  • By 2025, testers equipped with infrared thermal imaging modules will simultaneously perform "leak location" and "leak rate quantification";

  • Modular design shortens changeover time from 30 minutes to 5 minutes, meeting the demands of multi-variety small batch production.

3. Global Collaboration on Industry Standards

Currently, testing standards are fragmented across industries (e.g., automotive ISO 16750, medical ISO 10993), but future trends include:

  • Development of cross-industry universal testing protocols (e.g., OPC UA-based data formats);
  • Internationally recognized leakage rate calibration systems to resolve "result discrepancies among different testing institutions for the same product."

The value of high-precision airtightness testers has surpassed mere "quality gatekeeping," becoming a "data engine" for process optimization. A new energy battery manufacturer analyzed detection data and found a positive correlation between leakage rates and welding temperature in a batch of cells.
