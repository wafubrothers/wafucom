1. Identify the Core Requirements of Your Product

2. Understand the Mainstream Testing Technologies

Common testing methods on the market include direct pressure testing, differential pressure testing, and flow testing, as well as specialized helium mass spectrometry. Each method has its strengths‚Äîthere is no absolute ‚Äúbest,‚Äù only what best fits your product!

    1. Direct Pressure Testing

  • Principle: Air is directly charged into the product, and sensors monitor internal pressure changes to determine leakage.
  • Advantages: Low cost, fast testing speed, widely used, simple operation.
  • Disadvantages: Accuracy is easily affected by environmental noise and conditions.

    2. Differential Pressure Testing

  • Principle: The product is inflated to a target pressure, similar to controlling water flow from a tap. The system then measures how much the flow changes per minute; deviations indicate leakage.
  • Advantages: Fast detection speed, results are independent of the product‚Äôs internal volume, suitable for large-volume components.
  • Disadvantages: Requires a reliable reference part, and the testing process is relatively complex.

    3. Flow Testing

  • Principle: Air is charged into the product, and flow sensors measure pressure differences to determine leakage.
  • Advantages: High accuracy, quick detection, widely applicable.
  • Disadvantages: Higher cost compared to other methods.

    4. Helium Mass Spectrometry

  • Principle: The tested part is first evacuated, then filled with helium, which is detected using a helium mass spectrometer.
