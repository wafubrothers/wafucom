Case Study: Air Tightness Testing of Water Pump Motors

August 22, 2025

1. Why Do Water Pump Motors Require Air Tightness Testing?

During operation, if water enters the interior of a pump motor, it can easily cause short circuits, component damage, or even total equipment failure. Therefore, it is crucial to test the sealing performance of the motor housing and key joints in advance. Through air tightness testing, manufacturers can detect casting pores or welding defects in the motor housing, as well as evaluate the sealing performance of the upper and lower shell joints, ensuring every motor leaving the factory is reliable and durable.

Figure 1: Illustration of a Water Pump Motor

2. Main Testing Points for Water Pump Motors

In practical testing, there are several areas of a water pump motor that require inspection, such as:

  • Housing surface: Focus on whether the motor housing has pores, sand holes, or poor welding, preventing small defects from becoming water ingress pathways.
  • Upper and lower shell joints: This is the area most prone to air or water leakage. Testing ensures tight sealing at the joints to avoid leakage problems during long-term operation.
  • Other key interfaces: Areas such as cable entry points and around bearings also require air tightness testing to thoroughly eliminate potential risks.
